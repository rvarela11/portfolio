Description:

Fuel helps you compare your goal calories vs your daily calories in three simple steps. The first step is to get your goal calories. This is calculated by collecting some general information. The second step is to get your now calories. This is calculated by searching food items and placing them in a breakfast, lunch, or dinner box. The third step will give you a breakdown.

Technologies:

  • JavaScript
  • jQuery
  • NutritionX API

Challenge

One of the challenges was displaying the now calories number (top right) after a user had dropped a food item in the box. The challenging part was that the information provided by the API was a string that included the list item and the calories number.

To solve this, I added an equal sign between the food item and the calories. Next, I ran a replace method and replaced everything (including the equal sign) to the left of the equal sign with an empty string. This left me with only the number as a string. To convert it to an integer a ran it through a parse Int.

var calories = parseInt(item.replace(/.*=/, ""), 10);
